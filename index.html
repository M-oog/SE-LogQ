Abstract

Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) and Post-Training Quantization (PTQ) represent the two main approaches for efficient deployment of super-resolution (SR) models under low-bit quantization[cite: 60]. Unlike QAT, which requires retraining, PTQ is often preferred for its rapid deployment[cite: 61]. However, improving PTQ accuracy remains challenging due to two key issues: linear quantization grids poorly approximate the heavy-tailed activation distributions of SR models, and magnitude-based calibration metrics underrepresent low-contrast yet structurally critical features[cite: 62]. To address these issues, we propose SE-LogQ, a spectral entropy modulated logarithmic quantization framework[cite: 63]. Extensive experiments reveal that SE-LogQ outperforms state-of-the-art PTQ methods in reconstruction quality while incurring minimal computational cost[cite: 66].

Quantitative Results

SE-LogQ demonstrates superior performance across standard benchmarks, particularly in the challenging W4A4 setting[cite: 554]. On the Urban100 dataset using the RDN model, it achieves a PSNR of 25.98 dB and an SSIM of 0.783, with a Feature Average Bit-width (FAB) of 3.7[cite: 556, 411]. When applied to the EDSR model for a scale factor of 4, it reaches 25.73 dB PSNR and 0.772 SSIM[cite: 411].

Qualitative Results

Traditional linear uniform quantization methods often suffer from severe structural collapse and fail to reconstruct dense grid patterns[cite: 644]. In contrast, SE-LogQ effectively suppresses these artifacts, faithfully restoring continuous diagonal grids and preserving sharp, high-contrast edges[cite: 646]. Our entropy-driven strategy successfully targets subtle structures, adaptively allocating higher bit-widths to faint facial features, smooth gradients in skies, and low-luminance textures[cite: 647, 649].
